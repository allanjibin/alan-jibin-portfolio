Automated Metal Defect Detection.

A real-time quality control system utilizing camera vision and deep learning architecture to identify manufacturing flaws instantly.

The Challenge.

Developed as my core B.Tech project, this system addresses a critical bottleneck in manufacturing pipelines: manual quality inspection. Human visual inspection of metal surfaces is prone to fatigue-induced errors, is time-consuming, and limits production throughput.

The objective was to create a cost-effective, highly accurate automated system capable of detecting micro-flaws—such as scratches, dents, and surface irregularities—on metal parts moving along a production line in real-time.

The Approach & Technology.

The core of the solution relies on advanced Computer Vision techniques paired with a deep learning detection model.

  • Hardware Integration: High-resolution camera setups integrated into the inspection area to capture consistent lighting and angles of the metal surfaces.
  • Model Architecture: Utilized the YOLO (You Only Look Once) object detection algorithm due to its superior speed and accuracy in real-time environments, training custom weights specific to metallic textures.
  • Processing Pipeline: Wrote the backend logic in Python using OpenCV for image pre-processing before feeding the frames into the neural network for inference.

Current Status & Results.

The model successfully processes live video feeds, drawing bounding boxes around identified defects with high confidence thresholds. Future iterations will focus on cost optimization of the hardware rig and expanding the dataset to recognize a wider variety of alloy defects.
