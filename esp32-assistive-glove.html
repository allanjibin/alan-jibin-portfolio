C / C++ ESP32 Embedded Systems

Assistive Glove for Mute Communication.

A wearable IoT solution designed to translate hand gestures into audible speech and text using flex sensors and IMU data.

The Challenge.

Communication barriers significantly impact the daily lives of individuals with speech and hearing impairments. While sign language is highly expressive, it relies on the recipient also understanding the language, which is often not the case in public or emergency settings.

This project aims to bridge that gap by creating a portable, wearable device capable of reading physical hand gestures and translating them into standard text and audible speech in real-time.

Hardware & Architecture.

The system relies on a combination of analog and digital sensors processed by a powerful microcontroller to recognize complex hand movements.

  • Sensory Input: Flex sensors are attached to individual fingers to measure the degree of bending. An IMU (Inertial Measurement Unit) tracks the overall orientation, acceleration, and tilt of the hand in 3D space.
  • Microcontroller (ESP32): Chosen for its processing speed and built-in wireless capabilities. It reads the analog values from the flex sensors and the I2C data from the IMU.
  • Translation Logic: Written in C/C++, the embedded code maps specific combinations of sensor thresholds (e.g., index finger bent, hand tilted forward) to predefined characters or words.

Development & Calibration.

A major focus of the development involves debouncing sensor inputs and fine-tuning the resistance thresholds to ensure accurate gesture recognition without false positives. The ESP32's connectivity also allows for future expansion, such as transmitting the translated text directly to a paired smartphone app via Bluetooth.
